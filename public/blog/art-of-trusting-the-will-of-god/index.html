The Art of Trusting the Will of God

September 4, 2025 • 2 min read • 430 words

Reflections on surrender, patience, and finding strength in uncertainty.

Trust is not the absence of fear, but the decision to move with faith despite it. To trust the will of God is to accept that clarity often comes after commitment — that we walk first, and the path reveals itself while walking.

Surrender vs. Control

We try to control outcomes because uncertainty makes us anxious. But control is often an illusion. Surrender is not passivity — it is disciplined reliance. It is the quiet act of saying: I will do my part with excellence and leave the rest to the One who governs all outcomes.

Trust in Uncertainty

Trust grows in the unknown. When we cannot predict or guarantee, we learn reliance, prayer, and patience. Delay is not denial; sometimes it is training. Sometimes protection. Sometimes a redirection toward something better than what we asked for.

On Waiting and Comparison

I’ve lately felt like the things I want in life aren’t coming. Others have them, but I don’t—and I beat myself up for it. Why? If I say I trust the will of God, then my heart has to learn to wait without turning bitter, to try without measuring my worth by timelines and comparisons.

Practices That Help

  • Daily remembrance and prayer to re-center the heart
  • Gratitude journaling to notice subtle blessings
  • Taking the next right action without overplanning
  • Seeking counsel, then making a firm decision and not spiraling
  • Accepting outcomes with humility and hope

In the end, trusting God is not a single moment — it is a practice. A way of carrying yourself. A way of moving through life with steadiness. You show up, you try, you trust — and what is written meets you where you stand.

Inshallah.
